# Learner Modeling: Future Research Questions

## 1. Unified Representation

- What is the minimal sufficient set of dimensions for an effective learner model?
- Can cognitive, affective, and behavioral signals be represented in a unified latent space?
- How do we weigh dimensions when they conflict (e.g., high knowledge but low motivation)?

## 2. Temporal Dynamics

- How do we model learner state at multiple timescales (seconds, minutes, days, years)?
- What forgetting functions generalize across domains and learner populations?
- How do we distinguish temporary performance fluctuations from true knowledge state changes?

## 3. Transfer and Generalization

- Can learner model parameters learned in one domain inform another?
- What is the minimum amount of cross-domain data needed for effective transfer?
- How do we model domain-independent learner traits (e.g., grit, self-regulation) separately from domain-specific knowledge?

## 4. Open Learner Models and Agency

- How does learner access to their own model affect motivation and self-regulation?
- What level of model complexity can learners understand and productively edit?
- How do we prevent learners from gaming or distorting their own model?

## 5. Privacy-Preserving Modeling

- Can we maintain rich learner models while minimizing data collection?
- How do federated learning or differential privacy apply to learner modeling?
- What is the trade-off between model accuracy and privacy guarantees?

## 6. Fairness and Equity

- Do learner models introduce or amplify bias against demographic subgroups?
- How should fairness be operationalized in educational contexts (equal accuracy, equal opportunity, calibrated predictions)?
- Can learner models be used to detect and remediate systemic inequities?

## 7. Explainability

- What explanation formats are most useful for learners vs. educators vs. administrators?
- Can hybrid symbolic-neural models provide both accuracy and interpretability?
- How do we explain temporal changes in learner state?

## 8. Validation and Ground Truth

- How do we validate that a learner model accurately represents the learner's internal state?
- What is the role of external assessments vs. learning process data in validation?
- How do we measure the pedagogical impact of learner model accuracy?

## 9. Misconception Modeling

- Can misconceptions be automatically detected from interaction data?
- How do we model the persistence and decay of misconceptions over time?
- What is the relationship between misconception models and knowledge tracing models?

## 10. Lifelong and Cross-Context Learning

- How do we maintain a learner model across institutions, platforms, and life stages?
- What standards or formats enable learner model portability?
- How do we handle concept drift: the learner's goals and context change over time.

## 11. Multi-Agent Impact on Learner Models

- How does multi-agent tutoring affect learner state representation?
- Should different agents maintain separate learner model views, or a single shared model?
- How do we reconcile conflicting agent assessments of learner state?

## 12. Generative AI and Learner Models

- How do LLM-generated explanations and content interact with learner model updates?
- Can LLMs be used to generate synthetic but realistic learner models for training and testing?
- How do we detect and correct hallucinations in learner model inferences?
