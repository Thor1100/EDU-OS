# Future Questions: Cognitive Architectures Research

## Immediate Term (1-2 Years)

1. **How can cognitive architectures better integrate emotional and motivational components with cognitive processing?**
   - What are the minimal architectural extensions needed to model affective influences on memory, attention, and decision-making?
   - How should architectures model the bidirectional relationship between cognition and emotion?

2. **What is the optimal granularity and structure for working memory models in cognitive architectures?**
   - Should working memory be modeled as a limited number of slots, a continuous resource, or an attentional control system?
   - How do individual differences in working memory capacity map onto architectural parameters?

3. **How can cognitive architectures better account for the role of the body and environment in cognition (embodiment)?**
   - What architectural mechanisms are needed to tightly couple perception, action, and cognition?
   - How should architectures model affordance perception and skill-mediated environmental coupling?

4. **How can we develop more rigorous methods for validating cognitive architectures against neural data?**
   - What are the best approaches for mapping architectural components to brain systems and processes?
   - How should architectures incorporate neurophysiological constraints and findings?

5. **What architectural extensions are needed to better model individual differences in cognition?**
   - How should architectures parameterize working memory capacity, processing speed, and learning style differences?
   - How can architectures account for developmental, genetic, and experiential sources of variation?

## Medium Term (3-5 Years)

1. **How can we develop truly unified cognitive architectures that integrate perception, action, learning, and reasoning?**
   - What architectural principles best support seamless integration between traditionally separate cognitive modules?
   - How do we avoid the fragmentation problem where architectures excel in some domains but fail in others?

2. **What is the role of consciousness in cognitive architectures, and how should it be modeled?**
   - Which aspects of cognition require conscious processing versus which can occur unconsciously?
   - How should architectures model meta-consciousness, self-awareness, and reportability?

3. **How can cognitive architectures better model collective and distributed cognition?**
   - What architectural extensions are needed to model cognition that extends beyond the individual brain?
   - How should architectures incorporate tools, technologies, and social structures as cognitive extensions?

4. **How should cognitive architectures handle the framing and context dependence of cognition?**
   - What mechanisms explain how the same knowledge produces different inferences in different contexts?
   - How do architectures model context sensitivity and framing effects in reasoning and decision-making?

5. **How can we develop cognitively plausible architectures that scale to real-world complexity?**
   - What architectural principles support scaling from toy problems to real-world knowledge domains?
   - How do we manage computational complexity while maintaining psychological validity?

## Long Term (5-10 Years)

1. **How might advances in neuroscience fundamentally reshape our understanding of cognitive architecture?**
   - What emerging neuroscientific findings (connectomics, neural dynamics, glial functions) challenge current architectural assumptions?
   - How should architectures adapt to new understandings of brain organization and function?

2. **Can we develop cognitive architectures that learn their own structure and organization?**
   - What mechanisms would allow architectures to meta-learn their own cognitive organization?
   - How would such architectures balance innate structure with learned adaptation?

3. **How should cognitive architecture research address the hard problem of consciousness in educational contexts?**
   - What aspects of learning and education necessarily involve conscious experience?
   - How might architectural approaches to consciousness inform the design of educational technologies?

4. **What are the implications of cognitive architectures for artificial general intelligence (AGI) in education?**
   - How close are current cognitive architectures to supporting AGI-level educational reasoning?
   - What architectural breakthroughs would be needed for truly adaptive, general educational intelligence?

5. **How can cognitive architectures better model wisdom, judgment, and epistemic cognition in addition to intelligence?**
   - What architectural extensions are needed to model intellectual virtues, epistemic curiosity, and judgment under uncertainty?
   - How should architectures model the development of wisdom and sophisticated epistemic practices?

## EduOS-Specific Future Questions

1. **How can EduOS best integrate multiple complementary cognitive architectures rather than committing to a single approach?**
   - What mechanisms should EduOS use to switch between or combine different architectural models?
   - How should EduOS handle conflicting predictions from different cognitive architectures?

2. **What is the optimal balance between architectural fidelity and computational efficiency in EduOS implementations?**
   - Which architectural components provide the greatest educational value per computational cost?
   - How should EduOS approximate complex architectural mechanisms for real-time operation?

3. **How should EduOS handle the developmental trajectory of cognitive architectures across the learner lifespan?**
   - What mechanisms should EduOS use to detect and respond to developmental shifts in cognitive capabilities?
   - How should EduOS adapt its architectural assumptions for different age ranges and educational levels?

4. **How can EduOS leverage cognitive architecture insights to improve assessment design and interpretation?**
   - What assessment formats best probe the mechanisms postulated by cognitive architectures?
   - How should EduOS interpret assessment results through the lens of specific architectural theories?

5. **How should EduOS evolve to support emerging understandings of cognition as inherently social, cultural, and technological?**
   - What architectural extensions are needed to model cognition that is fundamentally distributed and collaborative?
   - How should EduOS incorporate cultural tools, technologies, and practices into its cognitive architecture?
