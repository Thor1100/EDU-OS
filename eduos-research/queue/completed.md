# Completed Research Topics

## 2026-06-19

- **Explainable AI and Open Learner Models** — Completed
  - Explainability techniques surveyed: LIME, SHAP, counterfactuals, attention visualization, LRP, concept bottleneck models, model distillation, and interpretable-by-design approaches.
  - Open learner model architectures analyzed: skill meters, knowledge maps, textual summaries, editable models, comparative views.
  - Relationship between transparency, trust, and learning outcomes examined; OLM effects are modest (d ≈ 0.2–0.4) and context-dependent.
  - Presentation methods evaluated: mastery heatmaps, progress rings, graph views, timelines, dashboards.
  - Tension between model complexity and explainability mapped across educational decision domains.
  - Ethical implications analyzed: right to explanation (GDPR), risks of transparency (gaming, stereotype threat, privacy), risks of opacity (loss of agency, bias, trust erosion), contestability requirements.
  - Architecture impact mapped across all eight EduOS subsystems (ADS, AOS, KGS, KIS, MAS, RCS, TAS, LMS) with new agents (Explanation, Contestability, Model Card), explanation workflows, compositional explanation generation, and open learner model infrastructure.
  - Deliverables: summary.md, critique.md, architecture_impact.md, future_questions.md.
  - Knowledge graph updated: new concepts (explainable-ai, interpretability, open-learner-model, lime, shap, counterfactual-explanation, attention-visualization, scrutability, contestability, trust-calibration, compositional-explanation, adaptive-explanation, model-distillation, layer-wise-relevance-propagation, concept-bottleneck-model, what-if-analysis), new relationships, and new open problems (P144–P153).

- **Affective Computing and Engagement Modeling** — Completed
  - Affective computing methods surveyed: physiological sensors, behavioral proxies, linguistic detection, and multimodal fusion.
  - Engagement modeling approaches analyzed: self-report, behavioral trace, deep learning, and integrated affective-engagement frameworks.
  - Foundational papers from Picard (1997), Csikszentmihalyi (1990), D'Mello & Graesser (2012), Pekrun et al. (2007), Conati & Maclaren (2009), and recent multimodal/LLM advances reviewed.
  - Theoretical foundations mapped: control-value theory, flow theory, broaden-and-build, multidimensional engagement (behavioral, emotional, cognitive).
  - Ethical and privacy considerations: surveillance risk, consent, cultural validity, emotional manipulation, and bias in affect models.
  - Critical gaps identified: sensor intrusiveness, reliability/generalizability across populations, ambiguity of behavioral proxies, lack of causal evidence, overemphasis on detection vs. intervention, and deep cultural bias.
  - Architecture impact mapped across all eight EduOS subsystems (ADS, AOS, KGS, KIS, MAS, RCS, TAS, LMS) with new affective agents, engagement monitoring, ethical guardrails, and learner agency.
  - Deliverables: summary.md, critique.md, architecture_impact.md, future_questions.md.
  - Knowledge graph updated: new concepts (engagement-modeling, multimodal-affect-detection, behavioral-proxy, psychophysiological-sensing, flow-state, disengagement-detection, affective-intervention, control-value-theory), new relationships, and new open problems (P137–P143).

  - Risks cataloged: hallucination, pedagogical misalignment, bias, safety/content risks, and epistemic/motivational risks.
  - Guardrail techniques surveyed: RAG, constraint enforcement, verification chains, output filtering, persistent learner model integration.
  - Safety frameworks analyzed: technical (moderation, sandboxing, canary), pedagogical (curriculum anchoring, misconception awareness, scaffolding gates), and regulatory/ethical (COPPA/GDPR, algorithmic accountability, HITL).
  - Evaluation benchmarks and red-teaming strategies cataloged.
  - Architecture impact mapped across all eight EduOS subsystems (ADS, AOS, KGS, KIS, MAS, RCS, TAS, LMS) with dedicated guardrail agents, safety pipelines, and escalation workflows.
  - Identified critical gaps: pedagogical harm undefined, guardrails trade safety for utility, deep bias unresolved, HITL doesn't scale, regulatory frameworks lag, long-term effects unstudied.
  - Deliverables: summary.md, critique.md, architecture_impact.md, future_questions.md, knowledge graph updated.
